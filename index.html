Reading

Currently Reading: Z: A Novel of Zelda Fitzgerald by Therese Anne Fowler

April 10, 2013 7:13 PM PST

Brief Synopsis: She has always been known as the girl behind the man, but truly she had almost as much influence over F. Scott's novels than he did! Although her righting gets overlooked, Zelda is a fast talking twenties girl like no other. Quick of wit, and strong of character, this book brings life to the person that is well deserved.

My Impressions so far: The dialogue that Zelda uses in this novel is so fast paced that you wonder if their brains worked differently back then. Her wit shines out with every sentence and word that she says, which makes it hard to get bored with this novel. And if one of the best characters I have ever read isn't enough, the story is amazing to! It chronicles the love and life of Zelda and F. Scott Fitzgerald. From love affairs, to competition with Hemingway, to insane asylums. Did I mention I love this book!?
